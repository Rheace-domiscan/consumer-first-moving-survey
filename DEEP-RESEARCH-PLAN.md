# Deep Research Plan
## Consumer-First Moving Survey Platform
**Date:** 2026-04-16

## Goal
Run a real multi-iteration deep-dive before locking any MVP.

## Research program

### Iteration 1, competitor landscape mapping
Goal:
- identify global competitors across mover-first, consumer-first, marketplace, inventory, and adjacent relocation tech
- create initial competitor matrix
- map product patterns, monetization, likely wedges

Outputs:
- competitor list by category
- initial matrix
- hypotheses about technology and business gaps

### Iteration 2, technology acceleration scan
Goal:
- identify the fastest credible technology stack for browser-first consumer surveys
- identify what can be bought, reused, or stitched together rather than invented
- compare tradeoffs among detection, segmentation, depth, SfM, tracking, mobile capture, and report generation

Outputs:
- stack options
- buy/build/borrow recommendations
- what reduces manual labeling and engineering drag

### Iteration 3, accuracy and defensibility pass
Goal:
- determine what actually improves measurement and inventory accuracy in practice
- identify where competitors may be weak
- separate “demo magic” from commercially reliable outputs
- define confidence model and human review strategy

Outputs:
- accuracy architecture
- validation/testing strategy
- where to aim for superiority versus where to stay practical

### Iteration 4, synthesis into practical MVP direction
Goal:
- combine research into a concrete MVP recommendation
- define phased roadmap with the fewest moving parts and highest commercial signal
- define what should explicitly wait until phase 2/3

Outputs:
- practical MVP recommendation
- phased roadmap
- competitor-informed positioning
- technology decision memo

## Loop-back rule
At the end of each iteration:
- identify weak spots in the research
- identify what still feels hand-wavy
- run another pass against those weak spots before concluding

## Standards for this research
- no shallow “top 5 tools” listicle thinking
- no assuming competitor tech without evidence or at least well-labeled inference
- explicitly distinguish:
  - confirmed facts
  - likely inference
  - speculation
- prioritize technologies that improve speed to MVP and operational accuracy
- avoid antique stacks and avoid custom training unless clearly justified
