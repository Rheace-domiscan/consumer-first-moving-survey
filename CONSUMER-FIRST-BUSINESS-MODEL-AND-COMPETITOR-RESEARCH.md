# Consumer-First Moving Survey Platform
## Business Model Rewrite, Revenue Options, Competitor Research, and Fast-to-MVP Technology Strategy
**Date:** 2026-04-16

---

## 1. Executive summary

This new direction is stronger and more differentiated than the original mover-first concept.

The business is no longer simply:
- software sold to removal companies

It becomes:
- a **consumer-led moving survey and move-intelligence platform**
- that **generates structured move data owned by the consumer first**
- and **monetizes on the mover/storage side when that data is unlocked and used**

That is a real strategic difference.

The clearest framing is:

**“A free consumer move survey passport that removal and storage companies pay to unlock.”**

This creates a wedge between:
- traditional lead-generation marketplaces like AnyVan and Compare My Move
- mover-first survey software like Yembo, Voxme, Move4U, and SurveyPlus

Your first paying customer is correct:
- **removal and storage companies**

Your first non-paying user is:
- **the consumer who is planning a move**

The product is:
- **survey tool first**
- **lead platform second**

That is the right order.

---

## 2. Clean rewrite of the business model

## Core concept
A consumer planning a move completes a guided self-survey on their phone.

That survey produces:
- room-by-room inventory
- estimated move volume
- special handling flags
- packing materials estimate
- optional media evidence
- structured quote-ready data package

The consumer then shares that survey to one or more removal/storage companies using:
- secure link
- claim code
- QR code
- email share

The mover/storage company then pays to unlock or use the full structured move package.

---

## 3. The product in practical terms

## Consumer side
The consumer experience is free.

They get:
- guided walkthrough explaining how to survey properly
- question flow with conditional logic
- video or multi-video upload/recording
- optional photo capture
- optional answers for loft, cellar, garden, sheds, garage, outbuildings
- ability to skip inaccessible areas and manually declare contents
- generated move summary
- ability to revisit, review, and reshare

The consumer outcome is not just “a quote request”.
It is a **portable moving data pack**.

### Data pack may include
- inventory list
- room grouping
- estimated total volume
- declared access notes
- fragile / special item flags
- recommended packing materials
- summary PDF
- structured JSON for integrations later

---

## Mover/storage side
The mover unlocks value, not just a lead.

They get:
- pre-qualified survey lead
- structured move data
- inventory and room summaries
- volume estimate and item review layer
- packing-material insight
- optional supporting video/photos
- quote-ready output
- later: integration to CRM/quoting systems

This is why the mover can justify paying.
You are not selling just a name and phone number.
You are selling **quote-readiness**.

---

## 4. Why this model is strategically good

## Strength 1: consumer creates the asset once
The customer scans once and can share multiple times.
That is a much better user story than repeating surveys with multiple firms.

## Strength 2: neutral layer positioning
At first, you can position as a neutral survey passport.
That gives trust and lowers user friction.

## Strength 3: stronger than basic lead-gen
Compare My Move and similar platforms mainly gather form input and distribute leads.
You can gather **much richer operational data**.

## Strength 4: stronger than mover-first survey SaaS
Yembo-style tools usually help the mover conduct surveys.
You are shifting creation of the data to the consumer side.
That is different.

## Strength 5: future platform optionality
Later this could grow into:
- quote comparison marketplace
- storage upsell layer
- packing materials commerce
- insurance and declarations layer
- move concierge

But none of that needs to be the MVP.

## 5. What problem the product is really solving

This is important because the answer is not just “AI moving survey”.

The consumer problem:
- I do not want to repeat my move details to many companies
- I do not want messy, vague quoting calls
- I do not know how much I need to pack or what materials are needed
- I want more control over the quote process

The mover problem:
- lead data is incomplete
- customers miss important items
- remote quotes are inaccurate
- surveyors waste time on weak leads
- quote turnaround is slow

So the real product promise is:

**“Create one structured move survey, then use it to get faster, better quotes and planning from movers.”**

## 6. Revenue model options

## Best initial rule
Do not charge the consumer at the start.

In UK removals, quote requests are expected to be free.
Charging the consumer early would create friction and kill adoption.

### Revenue option A: mover pays per unlocked survey
This is the strongest first model.

Example:
- consumer creates survey for free
- mover receives a preview or lead notification
- mover pays to unlock full details and media/data

Possible pricing bands:
- small local movers: £10 to £20 per unlock
- higher-value/full-house surveys: £20 to £50 per unlock
- premium leads or exclusive leads: more

### Why this is strong
- very simple to explain
- aligns payment with value received
- keeps consumer side frictionless
- easier to test quickly

### Weakness
If the surveys are poor or incomplete, movers will resist paying.
So product quality matters fast.

---

## Revenue option B: mover subscription + unlock usage
Example:
- £99 to £299/month base access
- includes limited survey unlocks
- extra unlocks billed separately

### Why this is strong
- creates predictable revenue
- supports serious mover accounts

### Weakness
Harder to sell before you have real inbound volume.

### Recommendation
Use this only after you have validated lead/survey quality.

---

## Revenue option C: matched quote / lead marketplace model
Example:
- consumer opts into quotes from multiple movers
- movers pay per matched lead, per unlocked survey, or per quote opportunity

### Why this is strong
- potentially much bigger scale
- more network effects
- closer to AnyVan / Compare My Move monetization

### Weakness
- messier business operationally
- more sales-heavy
- more complaints / lead disputes / exclusivity arguments
- risks making the survey product secondary

### Recommendation
Not phase 1.

---

## Revenue option D: storage/packing add-ons
Later revenue possibilities:
- storage lead fees
- packing material referral margin
- insurance upsells
- premium move planning bundle

These are useful later, not at the start.

---

## Recommended monetization sequence

### Phase 1
- consumer free
- mover pays per unlocked survey

### Phase 2
- mover subscription + unlock bundle
- optional preferred partner network

### Phase 3
- structured quote marketplace / matched quotes
- storage and service upsells

That sequence is clean and de-risks complexity.

## 7. Competitor landscape, consumer-first angle

## Key conclusion
Most existing players are either:
- mover-first survey software
- or broad lead-gen marketplaces

Very few appear to combine:
- consumer-owned self-survey
- portable structured move passport
- share-to-any-mover flow
- mover-paid unlock economics

That gap is the opportunity.

---

## A. UK marketplace-style competitors

### AnyVan
What they are:
- consumer-facing moving marketplace / booking platform
- instant pricing and matching for transport/removals/storage

What they are not:
- not a consumer-owned structured move survey passport
- not obviously a neutral portable survey handoff layer

Strategic relevance:
- major consumer acquisition competitor
- strong brand and SEO benchmark
- relevant because you want to beat them on consumer entry point

### Compare My Move
What they are:
- quote comparison / matching platform connecting consumers to verified companies

What they are not:
- not a deep survey or volumetric intelligence layer
- not obviously a portable structured survey data product

Strategic relevance:
- direct lead-gen comparison
- likely one of the closest business-model comparators on top-of-funnel demand capture

---

## B. Mover-first virtual survey competitors

### Yembo
- mover-first AI virtual surveys
- inventory generation from video
- sold to moving companies
- consumer is not the primary product owner

### Voxme Virtual Survey
- mover-first virtual video survey platform
- cloud recording, survey reports, mover branding
- mover workflow centric

### Move4U SurveyVideo
- mover-first branded survey call tool
- includes self-survey and video upload features
- still clearly sold to the moving company first
- includes ISO-style itemizer / estimate support

### Virtual Moving Technologies (VMT)
- virtual move survey service/tools
- offers DIY and survey-team-assisted modes
- seems closer to hybrid, but still centered on helping movers close business

### SurveyPlus
- virtual pre-move surveys conducted on behalf of moving/relocation companies
- service layer for movers, not consumer-owned survey passport

### Matthew James Removals / SurveyApp type examples
- individual movers sometimes offer self-survey app flows to their customers
- but these are mover-owned intake tools, not portable consumer-owned assets

But the dominant pattern is:

## 8. Competitive whitespace

The whitespace appears to be:

### 1. Consumer-owned portable move survey
The survey belongs to the consumer and can be reused across quote requests.

### 2. Structured move intelligence, not just a form lead
The asset is richer than a lead form.

### 3. Packing-material intelligence built in
Most competitors talk about quote support, video, inventory, or volume.
Less are obviously surfacing consumer-visible material requirements in a polished way.

### 4. Neutral-to-network transition model
Start neutral, then later gradually restrict or prioritize partner movers.
That is a plausible strategic sequence.

## 10. Fast-to-MVP technology strategy

You asked very specifically for speed.
You also said you do not want a technology path that creates unnecessary manual labeling burden or slows down iteration.
That is exactly right.

## Principle
The MVP should be built by combining:
- strong existing frontier models
- established product infrastructure
- minimal custom model training early on
- domain rules where rules are faster than model training

### This is the key mindset
Do not train your way into the MVP if you can product-engineer your way into it.

---

## Best fast-to-MVP stack

## Product stack
- web-first product surface
- mobile-responsive browser capture first
- optional native later if needed
- secure object storage
- background worker pipeline
- PDF + JSON outputs

## AI/CV stack for MVP
Use existing state-of-the-art pieces rather than inventing bespoke ML early.

### Recommended approach
1. object detection / segmentation from existing strong models
2. monocular depth / geometry priors from existing models
3. tracking and multi-frame fusion where practical
4. domain-specific rules engine for packing materials and special flags
5. human review layer for low-confidence cases

### Why this is right
You do not need a custom end-to-end model first.
You need:
- enough good detection
- enough good geometry for major items
- enough domain logic to make outputs useful

That is much faster.

---

## Specific technology candidates from prior research

### Detection / inventory
- YOLO-family models for speed
- segmentation-assisted detection for better object boundaries
- open-vocabulary or vision LLM support for weird edge items where useful

### Geometry / scale
- RoomPlan / LiDAR path on supported Apple devices later
- Depth Anything V2 for strong depth priors
- CoTracker3 for point tracking across frames
- VGGSfM for camera pose and scene structure when doing richer reconstruction

### Product infra
- WebRTC for live survey mode later
- browser upload/self-survey first
- queue-based frame processing and report generation

### Why this matters
This lets you move without committing to giant annotation projects in month one.

---

## 11. What should be rules-based first, not AI-first

To move fast, a lot should be rules-based in the first version.

### Use rules for:
- question branching logic
- loft / cellar / garage / shed conditional flows
- packing material estimation from item categories
- mattress bag logic
- TV box logic
- corner protection logic
- golf bag / lamp / mirror / artwork specialist packaging rules
- material recommendation templates

This is much faster than trying to learn everything from raw data.

### Example
If detected/declared:
- king mattress → 1 king mattress bag
- glass coffee table → corner protection + wrap flag
- wardrobe or many hanging clothes → wardrobe carton suggestion
- golf bag → specialist protection / sporting goods flag

These are domain logic wins.

---

## 12. What should not be overbuilt early

Do not overbuild these in phase 1:
- perfect full-property autonomous 3D reconstruction
- native app before browser MVP proves itself
- huge custom labeled dataset
- broad marketplace matching engine
- enterprise CRM integrations before quote-ready data is proven
- insurer/storage/claims extensions before mover-side value is proven

These are traps.
